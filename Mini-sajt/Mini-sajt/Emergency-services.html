NYPD

NYPD stands for "New York City Police Department" and is the primary local law-enforcment agency in New York City. It was created in 1845 and is currently United States' largest police department with around 55 000 employees in which 36 000 are sworn-in, uniformed police officers.

FDNY

FDNY stand for "Fire Department of the City of New York" is the main fire and rescue service of New York City. The Department was created in 1865 and is today the largest municipal fire deparment in the United States with a total of 11 000 firefighters employed plus an added 4000 EMS-personel.

FDNY Paramedics Department

FDNY does not only include firefighters but also 4000 EMS (Emergency Medical Services) employees, also called paramedics. The FDNY Bureau of EMS was established in 1996 following a merge of the FDNY and the New York City Health and Hospitals Corporation's Emergency Medical Services Division.
