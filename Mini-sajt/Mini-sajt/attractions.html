New York has many attractions to go to and many places to visit. Here are some of the most popular ones.



Empire State Building

The Empire State Building is one of New York most famous Landmarks. Construction of the building began in march 1930 and was finished May 1st 1931. The main part of the building is 381 meters tall, but including the mast it reaches to up to 443 meters. It has 102 floors and was ranked as the tallest building in the world from it's date of completion until the year 1972. From the top of the building you can go outside to their balcony at witness a 360 degree view of manhattan and it's sorrounding areas.




Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is yet another one of New York's most famous landmarks. Construction of this giant statue actually began in France during the early 1880s and was meant as a gift to the United States of America to celebrate the Franco-American Union that had been formed. A ceremony of dedication was held October 28th 1886 in New York. Thereafter the statue was open to the public. The Statue of Liberty stands on Liberty Island south of Manhattan. It stands on a pedestal making it 93 meters in height.




Times Square

Times Square is a major commercial intersection, tourist destination and entertainment hub in the middle of Manhattan. It consists of the blocks between 6th and 9th Avenue and 39th and 52nd street in Manhattan. It's center lies somewhere around 42nd Street and Broadway. It got it's name from the New York Times who previously had it's headquarters around here. Times Square is famous for it's huge brightly lit digital billboard running 24/7. One of the worlds bussiest pederstrain areas having 330 000 people passing through daily.




Central Park

Central park is in short, a giant park, in the center of Manhattan. It is 3,4 square kilometers big and stretches 50 streets between 59th street and 109th street. Central Park offers lots of activities to do, among those are, kayaking in the big lake, ice-skating, Central Park Zoo, rock-climbing, horse and carage, cycling, padelboats and concerts. Or you could of course just walk around, taking in the fresh air and greenery giving you a brake from the big city. It offers beautiful scenery like lakes, waterfalls, fountain, statues, grass and trees. All while seeing the big New York skyscrapers in the background.
